About Me

Robotics software engineer with 3+ years building production‑grade autonomous systems across humanoids, mobile robots, and robotic arms. I design, ship, and maintain complete ROS 2 stacks with clean interfaces and reliable real‑time control.

Vision-Guided Autonomous Docking Station

Developed a custom path planning algorithm integrated with a PID-tuned low-level controller for precise navigation and docking. Leveraged vision-based ArUco marker detection for robust station localization. The system autonomously monitors battery state, triggers docking when charge is low, and executes reliable obstacle-aware maneuvers to achieve seamless self-charging in dynamic environments.

Space Arm Docking Station Prototype

Developed a robotic arm prototype for in-orbit satellite servicing, where the arm is attached to a "chaser" satellite. The system autonomously planned a collision-free trajectory using the RRT* algorithm in ROS 2 to approach a "target" satellite. Final precise docking was achieved by visually servoing the arm's end-effector using an ArUco marker for real-time pose correction, successfully demonstrating a complete capture and dock sequence.

UR10e EOAT & Vision on Curved Surfaces

Worked on the UR10e robotic arm where I implemented a vision-driven path planning system to handle complex curved-surface operations. Using a 3D depth camera, I generated OctoMaps in RViz2 to accurately represent the environment and surface details. Based on this, I developed and executed surface path planning algorithms that enabled the robot to perform precise scrapping tasks to remove flue stains from rocket components. This project was carried out for ISRO, ensuring high precision, reliability, and adaptability for aerospace applications.

Vision-Guided Robotic Arm for Inspection

This project employs a robotic arm and OpenCV with a webcam to perform a critical, pre-paint quality inspection of car arm parts on the production line.
